The Problem

The materials we build with — steel and concrete — carry major shortcomings.

0% of global emissions come from steel & cement alone — while mining finite ores degrades ecosystems and fuels "sand wars" worldwide.

Concrete

  • Slow — requires a 7+ day curing process
  • Imprecise — shrinks and cracks
  • Unsafe — fragility leads to failure in earthquakes and salt conditions

Steel

  • Extremely heavy to transport and erect
  • Rusts easily — and is dangerous to work with
  • Geopolitical & price volatility

Human & Planet

  • Disconnection from natural materials raises stress & cortisol
  • Cognitive fatigue and sensory overstimulation
  • Climate risks from production threaten lives and property

The Breakthrough

The fibers that give wood its grain are stronger than carbon fiber.

After working at the frontier of carbon nanotubes, Prof. Liangbing Hu made the connection: the cellulosic nanocrystals inside ordinary wood outperform the average carbon fiber.
